Integration as the Reverse of Differentiation

$$\frac{d}{dx}\big[f(x)\big]=f'(x)\quad\Longrightarrow\quad\int f'(x)\,dx=f(x)+C$$
  • $f'(x)$ is the derivative of $f(x)$;   $f(x)$ is the anti-derivative of $f'(x)$
  • $C$ = arbitrary constant of integration вҖ” always include for indefinite integrals
  • $\displaystyle\int\cdots dx$ = "indefinite integral of вҖҰ with respect to $x$"

Basic Properties

PropertyFormula
Addition / Subtraction$\displaystyle\int\big[f(x)\pm g(x)\big]dx=\int f(x)\,dx\pm\int g(x)\,dx$
Scalar Multiple$\displaystyle\int kf(x)\,dx=k\int f(x)\,dx,\quad k\in\mathbb{R},\,k\neq 0$
вҡ Cannot split products or quotients: $\int x\sin x\,dx\neq\!\left(\int x\,dx\right)\!\!\left(\int\sin x\,dx\right)$. Also $\int xe^x\,dx\neq x\int e^x\,dx$.

4.4.2 вҖ” Powers: Even vs Odd Strategy Memorise

Integration by Substitution

в„№The substitution will always be given in exam questions when this method is required.

Procedure

  1. Let $u=g(x)$. Differentiate: $\dfrac{du}{dx}$. Rearrange for $dx$ in terms of $du$.
  2. Express all remaining $x$-terms using $u$ (using $x=g^{-1}(u)$ if needed).
  3. Integrate with respect to $u$.
  4. Substitute back: replace $u$ with $g(x)$ to express the answer in $x$.
Worked Example вҖ” Algebraic substitution

Use $u=1+2x$ to find $\displaystyle\int x(1+2x)^{11}\,dx$.

  • $u=1+2x\Rightarrow x=\dfrac{u-1}{2}$,   $\dfrac{du}{dx}=2\Rightarrow dx=\dfrac{du}{2}$
  • $=\displaystyle\int\dfrac{u-1}{2}\cdot u^{11}\cdot\dfrac{1}{2}\,du=\dfrac{1}{4}\int(u^{12}-u^{11})\,du=\dfrac{1}{4}\!\left(\dfrac{u^{13}}{13}-\dfrac{u^{12}}{12}\right)+C$
  • $=\dfrac{(1+2x)^{13}}{52}-\dfrac{(1+2x)^{12}}{48}+C$

$$\int_a^b f(x)\,dx=\big[F(x)\big]_a^b=F(b)-F(a),\qquad F'(x)=f(x)$$

Definite Integrals with Substitution

  1. Change limits: substitute $x=a$ and $x=b$ into $u=g(x)$ вҶ’ new limits $u_1,u_2$.
  2. Replace $dx$, integrand, and limits вҖ” all in terms of $u$.
  3. Integrate in $u$ with the new limits. No need to revert to $x$.
Worked Example

Use $t=\sqrt{x+2}$ to evaluate $\displaystyle\int_{-1}^{1}x\sqrt{x+2}\,dx$.

  • $x=-1\Rightarrow t=1$; $x=1\Rightarrow t=\sqrt{3}$.   $x=t^2-2$,   $dx=2t\,dt$
  • $=\displaystyle\int_1^{\sqrt{3}}(t^2-2)(t)(2t)\,dt=2\int_1^{\sqrt{3}}(t^4-2t^2)\,dt=2\!\left[\dfrac{t^5}{5}-\dfrac{2t^3}{3}\right]_1^{\sqrt{3}}=\dfrac{2}{15}(7-3\sqrt{3})$

Definite Integrals with IBP

$$\int_a^b u\,\frac{dv}{dx}\,dx=\Big[uv\Big]_a^b-\int_a^b v\,\frac{du}{dx}\,dx$$
Worked Example вҖ” $\displaystyle\int_0^1 xe^{2x}\,dx$
  • $u=x$, $\frac{dv}{dx}=e^{2x}$ вҶ’ $v=\frac{1}{2}e^{2x}$
